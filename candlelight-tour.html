About the Event

Step Into the Past by Candlelight

Join us for an intimate evening series at one of Queens' most treasured historic landmarks — the Vander-Ende Onderdonk House, the oldest surviving Dutch Colonial farmhouse in New York City.

Each gathering blends a guided tour of the historic home with live music, all illuminated by the warm, dancing glow of candlelight. Walk through centuries of history, hear stories of the families who called this place home, and let the music carry you back in time.

This is an experience designed for those who appreciate history, community, and the quiet magic of a flame-lit room. Whether you're a history enthusiast, music lover, or simply looking for something truly special — you are welcome here.

The Vander-Ende Onderdonk House

1820 Flushing Avenue
Ridgewood, Queens
New York, NY 11385

Built circa 1709, the Vander-Ende Onderdonk House is the oldest Dutch stone house still standing in New York City. Listed on the National Register of Historic Places, it sits at the border of Ridgewood and Bushwick and is managed by the Greater Ridgewood Historical Society.
